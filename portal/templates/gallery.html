SciFi Gun Thing - Sept 2020
This is my first proper attempt at finishing a Blender project in a long time. I'm reasonably happy with the outcome. It's clunky and makes little sense but it was a good excercise in dusting off my Blender skills.

I used a reference image of an old-timey revolver to model the handle and trigger guard with a subsurface workflow and then kinda just winged it for the rest of the concept. In my mind I wanted there to be flaps that would raise or otherwise animate when the gun was overheated or out of ammo.
It kinda ended up with an improvised feel where the classic revolver handle has been bolted to some new piece of technology.

G3A3 Rifle
With this model I tried to create something that exists in the real world and I'm happy with the result, I imported a reference image into Blender and modeled each piece over several evenings. I also did a block out to get the proportions from the reference image correctly.

I'm getting more comfortable with working with sub divided surfaces like on the stock and grip. It is a bit on the high-poly side, especially in said stock and grip object.

Overall its recognisable as the G3A3 rifle to those familiar and I learnt a good amount working on this start to finish.

G3A3 Rifle by Jack.McComiskey on Sketchfab

Dumpster and Bonus Trash
Not exactly thrilling, but often neccesary are these detail pieces for larger scenes. I made this asset as part of a larger scene I'm working on and seperated it out to highlight some of my first attempts at using Substance Painter.

In this scene I've included some example detail objects such as a basic bin bag, cardboard boxes and a wet pavement to set a sort of example scene.

The pavement may look a bit strange in the preview, start the model viewer and the texture will load correctly
